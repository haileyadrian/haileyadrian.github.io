Hailey Adrian

Psychology Major

My name is Hailey Adrian and I am a first year Psychology major at the University of California, Riverside. So far, I have taken introductory Psychology courses that have broadened my understanding of the subject and the various methods of research as well as different subfields of psychology there are. I am interested in many aspects of Psychology and value the emphasis that it places on the importance of dealing with mental health struggles in a professional way. I am extremely excited to further pursue a career in the field of Psychology. I am currently pursuing a bachelor’s degree; however, I plan to go on to obtain a higher level of education through graduate school. I am interested in clinical Psychology as well as school psychology and am interested in discovering what my future in Psychology may hold. In order to pursue my career in Psychology, I am involved in the International Honor Society for Psychology. This provides me with information on how to prepare for my future career and take advantage of opportunities that UCR has to offer for Psychology majors. It provides me with knowledge and experience that helps build my resume and gives me an upper hand early on in my college journey. In my future years as a student of UCR, I plan to join research labs in the field of Psychology that provides me with experience in the lab. In addition, next year, I will be starting my job as a Peer Educator to teach incoming freshmen about tips to help them succeed in their freshman year. This will allow me to use the knowledge and experience that I have gained from my freshman year in college, to help others. In terms of professional work experience, I am currently the Social Media Marketing Assistant for the nonprofit Sarah’s House Maternity Homes. I run the nonprofit’s social media account, create flyers, respond to online questions, market events and fundraisers, and manage its online presence. This provides me with experience in marketing, communication, and graphic design. It is also meaningful to me as it supports a meaningful cause, by providing housing and support to pregnant and parenting women experiencing unstable housing. In addition, I volunteer at Tiny Treasures Collective, a nonprofit thrift store whose proceeds benefit Sarah’s House Maternity Homes. This has given me cashier and customer service experience, helping to develop my practical as well as interpersonal skills. All of this prior experience, as well as the experience I have gained from my first year in college, and the things I plan to become involved in are helping to further my future and prepare me to work in a field of Psychology.
